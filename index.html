Our History

Kabanana Ward is a vibrant community located in Lusaka, Zambia. Established as a residential area in the 1980s, our ward has grown into a thriving neighborhood that represents the diverse spirit of Lusaka. Over the decades, we have witnessed tremendous growth and development while maintaining our strong sense of community and cultural heritage.

Today, Kabanana Ward is home to thousands of families who work together to create a safe, prosperous, and sustainable environment for current and future generations. Our community is characterized by its entrepreneurial spirit, educational focus, and commitment to environmental conservation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our community and how you can get involved

You can get involved by attending our monthly community meetings, joining our cleanup campaigns, volunteering for educational programs, or participating in our community garden project. Contact us through our website or visit our community center for more information.

Community meetings are held every first Saturday of the month at 10:00 AM at the Kabanana Community Center. We discuss ongoing projects, plan future initiatives, and address community concerns. All residents are welcome to attend.

You can report issues by calling our community hotline, sending an email through our contact form, or speaking directly with ward councilors during community meetings. We also have a suggestion box at the community center for anonymous feedback.

Our community center offers meeting spaces, computer access, educational workshops, health awareness programs, youth activities, and serves as a venue for community events. The center is open Monday through Saturday from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

To start a new initiative, present your idea at a community meeting, gather support from residents, and work with the ward committee to develop an implementation plan. We encourage innovative ideas that benefit the entire community and align with our values.
